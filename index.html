Recommended Attractions on Mykonos

Mykonos Windmills
Tell me more...

This well-known photography spot can be found in Mykonos City and is an iconic feature of Mykonos. The windmills were originally built in the 16th century by the Venetians but ceased production in the 20th century. The windmills are a great point for viewing Mykonos city.

Armenistis Lighthouse
Tell me more...

Looking for the perfect place to watch the sunset? Head up to the Armenistis Lighthouse situated on the north-western tip of the island. Built as a tribute to the maritime history of Mykonos, the lighthouse is still fully functional even today.

Little Venice
Tell me more...

Mykonos was once under Venetian rule (13th century) and Little Venice strongly resembles this Italian city. The colourful, two buildings in this area of Mykonos city are now home to trendy restaurants, bars and cafes, but they still make the perfect (and incredibly popular) selfie backdrop.

Panagia Paraportiani
Tell me more...

Found in Mykonos city, this church is among the most photographed in the world! It is located right by the see in the Kastro neighbourhood of the city. What is interesting about this church is that it is actually made of 5 small churches built on top, or next to one another.

Recommended Restaurants

Hippie Fish
Tell me more...

This beautiful waterside fish restaurant is one of the most famous gourmet restaurants on Mykonos. It was first built in the 1960 and rose to fame more so after being featured in the 1989 film Shirley Valentine. The food, service and setting is divine, but those things come with a high price tag of course.

Visit the Hippie Fish restaurant website to learn more and to book a table. www.hippiefish-mykonos.com/

Kounelas Fish Taverna
Tell me more...

Our favourite fish restaurant in Mykonos city. It has been there since 1967 and serves quality, fresh fish, squid, octopus, lobster and shellfish, as well as other dishes to suit all tastes, as more budget friendly prices.

Visit the link below to learn more and to book a table. www.touristorama.com/en/kounelas-fish-tavern-mykonos

Lefteris GrillHouse
Tell me more...

Found right by Ornos beach, this restaurant has been our favourite find and we have been back multiple times. Mykonos isn’t cheap, but the food at Lefteris GrillHouse is not only delicious with a home-made feel, but it is very reasonably priced too!

Visit the Lefteris GrillHouse restaurant to learn more and to book a table. www.grillhouse-lefteris.com

Bakalo Greek Eatery
Tell me more...

This treasure was recommended by our hotel waiter and it did not disappoint. Found on the edge of the city, this tiny restaurant offers incredibly tasty traditional Greek dishes.

Visit the Bakalo restaurant to learn more and to book a table. www.bakalo.gr/

Recommended Areas of Mykonos

Mykonos Town
Tell me more...

The main town on Mykonos (also known as Chora & Mykonos City) is a stunningly beautiful town made up by a made of thin, white washed lanes featuring cute boutiques, restaurants and bars. You can spend hours getting lost in the streets, or relax with a meal or a drink along the waterside of Little Venice looking at the windmills, or in the harbour.

Ornos
Tell me more...

The streets of tiny Ornos are lined with tavernas and hotels, with a beach to each side. Korfos beach to the north has strong winds and popular with kite surfers. Ornos Beach to the south is much more sheltered, with the beach lined with relaxed beach clubs, sun loungers and umbrellas.

Ano Mera
Tell me more...

This quiet inland village is the second largest on the island. It is home to the monastery Panagia Tourliani, as well as tasty local tavernas and cafes. There are many walking trails around the village providing beautiful views of the rocky landscapes and hills.

Platis Gialos
Tell me more...

This lively beachside village offers many waterfront bars, tavernas, restaurants and water sports, including yachting, windsurfing and parasailing. A great spot for a quick stop, or an all dayer.
